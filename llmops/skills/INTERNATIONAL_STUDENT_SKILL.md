# 🌏 JARVIS Skill — International Student Guide (QUT Brisbane)

## Skill Overview
This skill allows JARVIS to assist international students who are new to Brisbane and QUT.
JARVIS can answer questions about arriving in Australia, getting around Brisbane, setting up
essentials (SIM, bank, accommodation), understanding Australian culture, and succeeding at QUT.

**Trigger phrases:**
- "I just arrived in Brisbane"
- "How do I get from the airport?"

- "What do I need to know about QUT?"

- "How does public transport work?"
- Any question about settling in Brisbane or starting at QUT

## 1. 🛬 Arriving at Brisbane International Airport

### Incoming Passenger Card (IPC)
- Every international arrival must fill in the **Incoming Passenger Card (IPC)** — a government document tied directly to the student visa.
- Must provide: contact person's name, their phone/email, and Australian residential address.
- This contact person will be called by authorities in emergencies (accidents, medical).
- **WARNING:** Lying on the IPC has led to visa refusals and deportations. IPC records are retained and reviewed at every future visa status change (TR, PR, Citizenship).
- The IPC must be filled on every international arrival to Australia.

### Biosecurity (CRITICAL)
- Do NOT bring any food, seeds, plants, herbs, soil, or organic material into Australia.
- Items will be confiscated, fines imposed, and all foodstuff destroyed.
- This includes home spices, grandmother's recipes, snacks from home — leave them behind.
- Biosecurity video is shown on the aircraft before landing — watch it carefully.

## 2. 📱 Communications at the Airport

- Brisbane Airport has **free Wi-Fi** — use it to contact family on arrival.
- **eSIM options** (buy before travel): AloSIM, Airalo — convenient for immediate connectivity.
- **SIM stalls at airport** — availability depends on flight arrival time and day.
- Always opt for a **prepaid SIM** if unsure about plans.
- Passport is required for SIM purchase — visa status is verified via VEVO check.
- **Recommended carriers:** Telstra, Optus, Vodafone (all support 4G/5G — 3G does NOT work in Australia).
- **Vanity numbers tip:** Amaysim sells custom mobile numbers at reasonable prices (runs on Optus network).

| Option | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| QUT Airport Reception | FREE | 24hr service for new international students. Extra charges for family members and extra luggage. Contact: qut@belairlimos.com.au |
| Maxi Taxi (13Cabs App) | Varies | Best for heavy/large luggage — Toyota Hiace vans |

| Didi | Cheaper than Uber | Good budget alternative |
| AirTrain | ~$22 one-way | Expensive but direct to city |
| **$1 Hack** | **$1.00** | See below — requires minimal luggage |

### The $1 Airport Hack (Budget Option)
1. Take the **free Airport Shuttle bus** to DFO (Direct Factory Outlet)
2. From DFO, board the **590 Bus** to Toombul ($0.50) — board from the FIRST stop in the direction of travel
3. From Toombul Interchange, take a bus or train to your destination
4. Total cost: **$1.00**

> ⚠️ Not feasible late at night or when some bus operators are not running. Always use Google Maps to plan.

## 4. 🚆 Public Transport in Brisbane (Translink)

- All public transport in South East Queensland (bus, train, ferry) is **50 cents per trip** — permanently.
- Purchase a **Translink Go Card** at airport train station or Central Station in the CBD.
- Most buses, trains, and ferries accept **VISA/MasterCard** tap-on tap-off — no Go Card needed.
- A 10 cent pre-authorisation charge may appear on card — actual fare of 50 cents deducts next business day.
- Useful link: [Translink Map](https://translink.com.au/plan-your-journey/maps)
- **Always use Google Maps** to plan routes.

## 5. 🏠 Australian Address — Why It's Critical

- An Australian address is required for: IPC, SIM purchase, bank account, government services (Medicare, Photo ID, Driver's License).
- Can be a temporary address (hotel, Airbnb, hostel, relative's place) — update it later.
- **Important:** For bank cards and government correspondence (Medicare cards, Photo ID), use an address where you will definitely receive mail — do NOT use a hotel or Airbnb you'll be checking out of before the card arrives.
- **Virtual Mailbox alternative:** [MBE Virtual Address](https://www.mbe.com.au/virtual-address/) if no fixed lease address yet.

## 6. 🏦 Banking in Australia

### What You Need to Open an Account
- Australian mobile number
- Email address
- Australian address
- Visa grant letter
- VEVO visa status check (sometimes required)

### Key Facts
- Australian banks use **BSB + Account Number** (no SWIFT/IBAN for domestic transfers — confirm overseas bank supports BSB for international wire transfers).
- **Digital debit card** is issued immediately — link to Apple/Google Wallet right away.
- **Physical card** arrives via Australia Post in 1–2 weeks.
- **Credit cards:** NOT available until you have a job and 3–6 months of payslips. Forget it for now.

### ATM Confusion (Australian Quirk)
- When withdrawing or depositing cash at ATMs, always select **"Savings"** — even though it's actually your Checking/Transaction account. This is a legacy of the Australian EFTPOS system.

### Forex / Currency Exchange
- If exchange rate is poor at home, bring USD, EUR, or GBP.
- Exchange at: **Prosegur Change** (Brisbane Airport International Terminal) or **Travelex** shops in the city.
- Some banks offer USD accounts — transfer to AUD when the rate is good.

### Branchless Banking (Fastest Option)
Open an account entirely via mobile app — no branch visit needed:
- [ANZ Plus](https://www.anz.com.au/plus/)
- [UBank](https://www.ubank.com.au/)
- [UpBank](https://www.upbank.com.au/)

Upload passport, visa, proof of address, and Australian mobile number to get started.

### Commonwealth Bank Warning
CommBank offers account opening 14 days before arrival — but the account is **frozen** until you visit a branch in person to unfreeze it. Only advantage: ATM card is ready on arrival.

## 8. 🦘 Australian Culture Shock Guide

### Power & Electrical
- Australia uses **220–240V AC 50Hz** with **Type I plugs** (3 flat pins).
- If your devices are rated for **110V** (USA, Japan), you need a **voltage converter** — not just an adapter.
- Plugging 110V devices into 240V without a converter will destroy your electronics instantly.

### Cigarettes & Tobacco
- One of the most expensive in the world: **$40–$60 AUD per pack of 20**.
- Heavy government tax discourages smoking. Good time to quit!

### Barefoot Culture
- Australians commonly walk barefoot in shopping centres, supermarkets, and public areas — especially in Queensland's warm climate. Don't be alarmed.

### Cost of Living (Price Guide)

| Item | Approximate Cost |
|---|---|
| Coffee (café) | $4.50–$7 |
| Dining out (casual) | $20–$35 per meal |
| Cinema | $15–$25 per ticket |
| Haircut (men) | $30–$60 |
| Haircut (women) | $60–$150+ |
| Gym membership | $15–$80/week |
| Domestic flights | $100–$300 |
| 7-Eleven coffee | Cheapest in city — fills to the brim |
| McDonald's ice cream cone | $0.50 |

### Budget Tips
- Cook at home as much as possible.
- Use student discounts everywhere.
- Shop at **KMart** and **Big W** for budget clothing, household items, toiletries, electronics.
- Use **Everyday Rewards** (Woolworths) and **Flybuys** (Coles) apps for discount points.
- **Costco** membership for bulk buying.
- Free activities: beaches, parks, hiking trails, Brisbane markets.

### Other Cultural Notes
- **Tipping is NOT expected** — service staff earn proper wages (unlike USA).
- **Shops close early** — most close 5–6 PM weekdays, 9 PM on Thursdays (late night shopping).
- **No jaywalking** — Australians genuinely wait for the green pedestrian light.
- **Wildlife** — possums, wallabies, snakes are real. Stay calm and keep distance.
- **No bargaining** — prices are fixed everywhere except markets and car yards.
- **No bidets** — toilet paper is standard. Plan accordingly.
- **Only two major supermarkets:** Woolworths (Wooleys) and Coles.

## 9. 🎓 QUT Student Success Guide

### Canvas — Your Academic Hub
Everything academic lives on Canvas:
- Unit/subject syllabuses and coordinator contacts
- All assignments, quizzes, and exam details
- Group project formation and submissions
- Rubric scoring, assignment grades, and final exam marks
- Live lectures recorded and available via **Echo360**

> **Pro Tip:** Install the Canvas app on your mobile — review content during your commute.

### Academic Success Tips
- Attend **all lectures in-person** even though they're recorded — your parents paid for you to be there.
- **Tutorials are mandatory** — attendance is recorded and submitted to the government.
- Take notes in every class, paying attention to topics emphasized by professors.
- Use your **QUT university account** to install Microsoft Office and OneDrive — store all study materials on OneDrive so nothing is lost if your laptop is damaged.
- Install on your mobile: **Microsoft Authenticator, QUT App, Canvas, Rubric App**.
- Never procrastinate — start assignments as soon as they are released.
- Review tutorial solutions leading up to the deadline — they often hint at assignment content.
- Part-time work is legal but limited to **4 hours per day** under student visa conditions.

### Academic Integrity Warning
- **Never use AI or outsource your assignments.**
- QUT uses **Turnitin with AI detection tools**.
- Cheating is reported to the Academic Integrity Council — professors also use AI to detect plagiarism.

### Unique Student Identifier (USI)
- USI **must be created while in Australia** — cannot be done after departure.
- Required documents: non-Australian passport with Australian visa, Brisbane address, Australian mobile number.
- Use your **personal email** (not QUT email) to create a USI at [usi.gov.au](https://www.usi.gov.au/students/international-students-and-students-studying-overseas).
- After creation, log in to the QUT Portal and update your profile with the USI code.
- **Warning:** You may not be able to graduate or receive your qualification if you leave Australia without creating a USI.

### QUT ID Card
- Get a **physical QUT ID card** from HiQ — digital ID in the QUT app is not always accepted.
- Physical ID is required to enter the Library during non-teaching periods.
- Carry your QUT physical ID everywhere instead of your passport.
- Consider getting a **Queensland Proof-of-Age / Photo ID card** if you don't plan to get a driver's licence.

### Wi-Fi on Campus
- Connect to **QUT Wi-Fi** on campus.
- Use **eduroam** with your QUT credentials — works at any university across Australia that has eduroam.

## 10. 🚌 Getting to QUT Campuses

### Gardens Point (GP) Campus — City Campus
| Method | Details |
|---|---|
| Ferry | GP has its own ferry terminal — catch a ferry directly to campus |
| Free City Loop Bus 40 | Clockwise route — drops off at GP, $0.00 (do NOT tap on/off) |
| Free City Loop Bus 50 | Anticlockwise route — drops off at GP, $0.00 (do NOT tap on/off) |
| Train | Nearest station: **South Bank** — walk across Goodwill Bridge to GP |
| Train (alternative) | **Central Station** — walk through Queen Street Mall to GP |

### Kelvin Grove (KG) Campus
| Method | Details |
|---|---|
| Metro Bus | Latest electric Metro Bus stops at KG bus terminal |
| Train + walk | Nearest station: **Normanby** — long walk or connect via bus |
| QUT Shuttle | Free shuttle bus between GP and KG campuses |

### Campus Facilities
- **GP Campus:** Heart of the city — nearby restaurants, cafes, malls, cinema. EzyMart on campus for affordable machine coffee.
- **KG Campus:** Has a small "Village" with cafes, stores, and restaurants. EzyMart on campus too.
- **Both campuses** have food courts.
- **Brisbane Weekend Flea Markets:** GP students → Botanic Gardens market or Brisbane Square Library Markets. KG students → The Village market.

- Give exact costs, app names, and step-by-step instructions.
